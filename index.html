Chicago Address Conversion Site

The first way I'm looking to do this is by creating a database of the address conversions that happened in Chicago in 1909. That year, the entire city went from its previous addresses to the order we're familliar with today. If you want to figure out the old address of a building, you have to hunt through a PDF scan of a book from 1909 listing every street and its new/old address. My first project as a developer is to create a website in which researchers like myself can simply enter in the new (or old) address to find its partner without having to scan through an old PDF.

It's a simple idea that requires a lot of data entry, but with fellow historians to help enter addresses, it'll be a site that ultimately saves time for researchers and allows easier access for casual historians to look up the history of a specific building. With skills I'll learn in Python and basic front end development, I'm excited to bring the site to life by the end of the summer. This will turn into a card that links you to lower on the page.
